There is a function \(f:\mathbb{R}\rightarrow\mathbb{R}\) such that \(f\) is continuous at every irrational number and discontinuous at every rational number.
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\begin{cases}0&x\in \mathbb{R}\setminus\mathbb{Q}\\ 1/q & x=\frac{p}{q},p,q\in\mathbb{Z},q\geq 1,\gcd(p,q)=1\end{cases} \end{equation*}
Note: \(f(0)=1\) since \(0=\frac{0}{1},\gcd(0,1)=1\text{.}\)
