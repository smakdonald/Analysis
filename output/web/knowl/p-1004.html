Suppose \(f:[-1,1] \longrightarrow \mathbb{R}\) is a bounded function that is continuous at 0. Let \(\alpha(x)=-1\) for \(x \in[-1,0]\) and \(\alpha(x)=1\) for \(x \in(0,1]\text{.}\) Prove that \(f \in \mathcal{R}(\alpha)[-1,1]\text{,}\) i.e., \(f\) is Riemann integrable with respect to \(\alpha\) on \([-1,1]\text{,}\) and \(\int_{-1}^{1} f d \alpha=2 f(0)\text{.}\)
