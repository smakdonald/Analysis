Continuous functions are not necessarily Riemann-Stieltjes integrable! Let \(f(x)=x\) and
\begin{equation*} g(x)=\begin{cases} 1 & x\in \mathbb{Q}\\ 0 & x\notin\mathbb{Q} \end{cases} \end{equation*}
Then \(g\notin\mathcal{R}([0,1])=\mathcal{R}([0,1],f)\) so by ibp \(f\notin \mathcal{R}([0,1],g)\text{.}\)
