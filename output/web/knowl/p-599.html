If \(f,g\in\mathcal{R}[a,b]\) and \(c,d\in\mathbb{R}\text{,}\) then \(cf+dg\in\mathcal{R}[a,b]\) and
\begin{equation*} \int_a^b(cf+dg)(t)\,dt=c\int_a^b f(t)\,dt+d\int_a^bg(t)\,dt. \end{equation*}
In other words, \(\mathcal{R}[a,b]\) is a vector space (over \(\mathbb{R}\)) and the function \(\mathcal{R}[a,b]\rightarrow\mathbb{R}\) where \(f\mapsto\int_a^b f(t)\,dt\) is linear.
