Theorem 2.12.

If \((x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty},(y_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}\subseteq \mathbb{C}\text{,}\) \(x_n\rightarrow x\in\mathbb{C}, y_n\rightarrow y\in \mathbb{C}\) then
  1. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}(x_n\pm y_n)=x\pm y\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}(x_ny_n)=x y\)
  3. If \(y\neq 0,\) exists \(k\in\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 1}\) such that for all \(n\geq k\text{,}\) \(y_n\neq 0\) and sequence \((\frac{x_n}{y_n})_{n=k}^{\infty}\) converges to \(\frac{x}{y}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}|x_n|=|x|\)
  5. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\overline{x_n}=\overline{x}\)
  6. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\text{Re}(x_n)=\text{Re}(x)\)
  7. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\text{Im}(x_n)=\text{Im}(x)\)
