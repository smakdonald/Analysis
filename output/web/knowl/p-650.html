Let \(f_n:[0,1]\rightarrow\mathbb{R}\) by \(f_n(x)=\begin{cases} n & 0<x<\frac{1}{n}\\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}.\) Then
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\rightarrow \infty}f_n(x)=0 \end{equation*}
for all \(x\in[0,1]\) and
\begin{equation*} \int_0^1f_n(x)\,dx=1 \end{equation*}
for all \(n\geq1\) so
\begin{equation*} 1=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\int_0^1f_n(x)\,dx\neq \int_0^1\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}f_n(x)\,dx=0. \end{equation*}
