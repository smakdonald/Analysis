Solution 4.88.1.

Fix \(\varepsilon>0\text{.}\) Set \(\delta=\frac{\varepsilon^2}{4}\text{.}\) Fix \(x,y\in[0,\infty)\) with \(\left|{x-y}\right|<\delta\text{.}\)
Case 1: If \(x<\delta\) and \(y<\delta\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \left|{\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{y}}\right| \leq \sqrt{x}+\sqrt{y} <2\sqrt{\delta} =\varepsilon \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Case 2: Suppose \(x\geq \delta\) or \(y\geq \delta\text{.}\) Wlog, \(x\geq \delta\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \left|{\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{y}}\right| =\left|{\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{y}}\right|\frac{\sqrt{x}+\sqrt{y}}{\sqrt{x}+\sqrt{y}} =\frac{\left|{x-y}\right|}{\sqrt{x}+\sqrt{y}} <\frac{\delta}{\sqrt{\delta}} =\sqrt{\delta} =\frac{\varepsilon}{2} <\varepsilon. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
