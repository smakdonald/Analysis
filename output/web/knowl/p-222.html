For every sequence \((x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}\subseteq \mathbb{R}\) there are subsequences \((x_{m_k})_{k=1}^{\infty}\) and \((x_{n_k})_{k=1}^{\infty}\) of \((x_{n})_{n=1}^{\infty}\) so that
\begin{equation*} \lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}x_{m_k}=\liminf_{n\rightarrow\infty}x_n \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}x_{n_k}=\limsup_{n\rightarrow\infty}x_n \end{equation*}
(these could be \(\pm\infty\)).
