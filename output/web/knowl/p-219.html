(\(\impliedby\)) is trivial. \((x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}\) is a subsequence of itself.(\(\implies\)) and “moreover”: assume \((x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}\) converges and write \(x=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}x_n\text{.}\) Let \((x_{n_k})_{k=1}^{\infty}\) be a subsequence. We'll show \(\lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}x_{n_k}=x\text{.}\)By the previous result,
\begin{equation*} x=\liminf_{n\rightarrow\infty}x_{n}\leq \liminf_{k\rightarrow\infty}x_{n_k}\leq \limsup_{k\rightarrow\infty}x_{n_k}\leq \limsup_{n\rightarrow\infty}x_{n}=x. \end{equation*}
So \(\liminf_{k\rightarrow\infty}x_{n_k}=\limsup_{k\rightarrow\infty}=x\) and hence \(\lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}x_{n_k}=x\text{.}\)
