Theorem 6.10.

Let f and g be continuous real-valued functions on a metric space M. Then
  1. |f| is continuous on M.
  2. f + g is continuous on M.
  3. cf is continuous on M for each c\in\R.
  4. f − g is continuous on M.
  5. f·g is continuous on M.
  6. f/g is continuous on M if g(x) ≠ 0 for every x\in M.
