Theorem 5.24. Borel-Lebesgue Theorem.

For a metric space \(X\text{,}\) the following are equivalent:
  1. \(X\) is compact.
  2. \(X\) is complete and totally bounded.
  3. Every open cover of \(X\) has a finite subcover. More often how compactness is defined.
  4. Every family of closed subsets of \(X\) with the finite intersection property has nonempty intersection.
  5. Every infinite subset of \(X\) has a cluster point.
