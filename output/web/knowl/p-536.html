One can also define derivatives on functions
\begin{equation*} f:\mathbb{C}\rightarrow\mathbb{C} \end{equation*}

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} f'(a) =\lim_{x\rightarrow a}\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}, \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
but the theory is much different. In some sense, not many functions on \(\mathbb{C}\) are differentiable.
